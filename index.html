Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our veterinary services

We recommend annual health checkups for young, healthy pets. Senior pets (7+ years) or pets with health conditions may need checkups every 6 months. Regular checkups help detect potential health issues early when they are most treatable.

Vaccination requirements vary by species, age, and lifestyle. Core vaccines for cats include rabies and feline distemper. Dogs typically need rabies, distemper, parvovirus, and adenovirus vaccines. Our veterinarians will create a customized vaccination schedule based on your pet's specific needs.

We typically recommend spaying or neutering between 4-6 months of age for cats and 6-9 months for dogs, but this can vary based on breed and individual development. Early spaying/neutering helps prevent unwanted litters and can reduce certain health risks and behavioral issues.

For emergencies during clinic hours, please call ahead if possible (01999-727389) so we can prepare for your arrival. After hours, call the same number and you'll be directed to the on-call veterinarian. Never hesitate to seek emergency care if your pet is experiencing distress.
